### Mathematical review of `IsStanleySeq`

The definition captures four properties: (1) `a 0 = 0`, `a 1 = n`; (2) strict monotonicity; (3) the image is 3-AP-free; (4) greediness — every skipped value would create a 3-AP.

The greedy condition (line 42–43) only checks APs of the form `(a i, a j, m)` where `m` is the largest term (`a i + m = 2 * a j`). This is correct: since `m > a k` (the current largest element) and all existing terms satisfy `a l ≤ a k < m`, the cases where `m` is the middle or smallest term of a new AP are impossible by arithmetic (they would require `m ≤ a k`, a contradiction). So the single case checked suffices.

The greedy condition starts at `k ≥ 1`, correctly reflecting that the initial pair `{0, n}` is given (not greedily chosen).
