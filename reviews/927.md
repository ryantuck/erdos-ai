  where `P` formalizes the original conjecture. The answer is `False` since Spencer disproved it.

  Instead, the file formalizes Spencer's disproof result (a lower bound `g(n) ≥ n − ⌊log₂ n⌋ − C`) as a direct assertion (Case 3) without any `answer()` wrapper.

  **Possible justification:** Formalizing the original conjecture faithfully would require defining the iterated logarithm (log\*) and expressing asymptotic O(1) equality, both of which are nontrivial. The current formalization captures the key mathematical content of Spencer's disproof. However, strictly speaking, the checklist requires `answer()` for yes/no problems.
