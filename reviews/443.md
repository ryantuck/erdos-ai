The docstrings phrase them as assertions ("The intersection can be arbitrarily large", "the intersection has subpolynomial size") rather than yes/no questions. This is a reasonable decomposition but deviates from the strict letter of the checklist.

  If `answer()` were required, the `(s : ℕ)` parameter on `erdos_443` would also violate the rule that quantifiers must go **after** `answer(sorry)`, not as theorem parameters. In the current Case 3 formulation, having `s` as a parameter is acceptable.
