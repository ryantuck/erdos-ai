### `answer()` usage

The original problem asks "For which $d$ must $A$ contain a three-term arithmetic progression?" — this could be read as **Case 2** ("for which", asking for specific values). However, the answer is fully known (d ≤ 3: yes, d ≥ 4: no), and the formalization decomposes the problem into two direct assertions that together characterize the complete answer. This is a standard and clean approach.

- [x] `erdos_192` (line 61): Direct assertion `∀ w : ℕ → Fin 3, HasAbelianSquare w` — **Case 3** (assertion that every 3-letter word has an abelian square). No `answer()` needed.
- [x] `erdos_192.variants.four_letters` (line 74): Direct assertion `∃ w : ℕ → Fin 4, ¬HasAbelianSquare w` — **Case 3** (assertion that a 4-letter abelian-square-free word exists). No `answer()` needed.
