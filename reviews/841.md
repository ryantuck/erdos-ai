## Mathematical Fidelity

The formalization is faithful to the source problem on erdosproblems.com/841:

- **$t_n$ definition:** Correctly captures the minimal $t$ such that $\{n+1,\ldots,n+t\}$ contains a nonempty subset whose product with $n$ is a perfect square, with $t_n = 0$ for square $n$.
- **Trivial lower bound** ($t_n \geq P(n)$): Matches the known result mentioned on the problem page.
- **Selfridge's result** ($t_n = P(n)$ when $P(n) > \sqrt{2n}+1$, and $t_n \ll \sqrt{n}$ otherwise): Correctly formalized from [ErSe92].
- **BPZ lower bound** ($t_n \gg (\log\log n)^{6/5}(\log\log\log n)^{-1/5}$): Correctly formalized from [BPZ24].
