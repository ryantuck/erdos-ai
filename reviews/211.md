The original problem states a direct assertion: "Given n points in ℝ², at most n−k on any line, there are ≫ kn many lines containing at least two points." This is a bound/assertion ("there are ..."), not a yes/no question or a request for a specific value. **Case 3** applies.

**Mathematical correctness note:** The "≫ kn" asymptotic notation is correctly captured by existential quantification over a positive constant C with the bound `lineCount S ≥ C · k · |S|`. The condition "at most n−k on any line" is formalized as ∀ distinct p, q ∈ S, `pointsOnLine S p q ≤ |S| − k`, which correctly bounds collinearity on every line determined by two points of S. The `lineCount` definition correctly counts distinct lines (as sets) containing ≥ 2 points via the image of `lineThrough` over distinct pairs, properly deduplicating since `lineThrough p q = lineThrough q p` as sets.
