## Mathematical Accuracy

The formalization is faithful to the original problem:

- **`ContainsSubhypergraph H k m`** correctly captures "$k$ vertices spanning at least $m$ edges": it requires existence of a vertex set S of cardinality k such that the number of edges of H contained in S is at least m.
- **3-uniform constraint** (`∀ e ∈ H, e.card = 3`) correctly enforces that every edge has exactly 3 vertices.
- **Edge count** (`H.card ≥ n ^ 3 + 1`) matches the original threshold of $n^3 + 1$.
- **Vertex set** `Fin (3 * n)` correctly models $3n$ vertices.
- **Disjunction** `ContainsSubhypergraph H 4 3 ∨ ContainsSubhypergraph H 5 7` matches the two alternatives in the original problem.
- **`answer(False)`** is correct: Harris's counterexample (28 edges on 9 vertices, i.e., n=3) disproves the conjecture.
- **Edge case n=0:** `Fin 0` is empty, so H is empty, and `H.card ≥ 1` is false — the implication holds vacuously. No issue.

**One subtlety:** `H : Finset (Finset (Fin (3 * n)))` models the hypergraph as a set of edges (finite sets of vertices). This does not enforce that edges are distinct beyond what `Finset` already provides (elements are unique). This is the standard representation and is correct.
