### `answer()` usage

The original problem asks: *"Is it true that c(n) ≫ n^n?"* — this is a yes/no question (**Case 1**).

- [x] Theorem type is `answer(sorry) ↔ P`. Correct for Case 1.
- [x] The RHS formalizes the question as asked: the existence of C > 0 and N₀ such that for all sufficiently large n, the threshold c(n) ≥ C · n^n. A "yes" answer (answer(True)) would mean c(n) does grow at least as fast as n^n.
- [x] Quantifiers go **after** `answer(sorry)` (they are inside the RHS of `↔`), not as theorem parameters.

**Formalization correctness:** c(n) is defined implicitly as the minimal m such that ∀ k ≥ m, the unit n-cube can be decomposed into k cubes. The formalization universally quantifies over all such m, which is equivalent to bounding c(n) since: (1) c(n) itself satisfies the hypothesis, so the bound applies to c(n); and (2) any m satisfying the hypothesis must have m ≥ c(n) (otherwise minimality of c(n) would be violated). The formalization is mathematically correct.
