## Formalization Assessment

The formalization captures the problem faithfully:

- **Clique-free / independent-set-free condition:** `G.CliqueFree ⌈10 * Real.log (↑n)⌉₊` and `Gᶜ.CliqueFree ⌈10 * Real.log (↑n)⌉₊` correctly encode "no clique and no independent set of size ≥ 10 log n" (complement clique = independent set).
- **Induced regular subgraph:** `∀ v ∈ S, (S.filter (G.Adj v)).card = d` correctly states that every vertex in S has exactly d neighbors within S under G, making the induced subgraph on S a d-regular graph. Since `SimpleGraph.Adj` is irreflexive, v is never counted among its own neighbors — this is correct.
- **Neither empty nor complete:** `d ≥ 1` (each vertex has at least one neighbor, so not an empty graph) and `d + 1 < S.card` (degree < |S| − 1, so not a complete graph). Correct.
- **Size bound:** `(S.card : ℝ) ≥ c * Real.log (↑n)` for some `c > 0`. Correct.
- **Asymptotic formulation:** Uses `∃ N₀, ∀ n ≥ N₀` to handle the asymptotic nature of the claim. Correct.

**Minor note:** `Real.log` is the natural logarithm. The constant 10 in the problem is tied to the choice of log base. This is a standard formalization choice and does not affect correctness since the problem's specific constant comes from the source.
