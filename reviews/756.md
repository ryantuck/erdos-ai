## Mathematical Notes

- `pairCount` counts **ordered** pairs `(x, y)` with `x ≠ y` at distance `d`. The docstring is explicit about this. The problem on erdosproblems.com says "more than n many pairs" without specifying ordered vs. unordered. Since each unordered pair `{x, y}` yields two ordered pairs `(x, y)` and `(y, x)`, the threshold `A.card < pairCount A d` (i.e., `n < ordered count`) is equivalent to `n/2 < unordered count`. If the intended threshold is `n` unordered pairs, the condition should be `2 * A.card < pairCount A d`. This is a mathematical interpretation choice — the formalization is internally consistent but worth verifying against the source paper.

- The interpretation of "≫ n" as "≥ c·n for some constant c > 0 and all sufficiently large n" (i.e., Ω(n)) is standard and correct.
