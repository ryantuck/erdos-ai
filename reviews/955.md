## Formalization Accuracy

The source problem ([erdosproblems.com/955](https://www.erdosproblems.com/955)) asks: if A ⊆ ℕ has natural density zero, must s⁻¹(A) also have natural density zero, where s(n) is the sum of proper divisors of n?

- **`sumProperDivisors`**: Correctly defined as `n.properDivisors.sum id`, using Mathlib's `Nat.properDivisors`. This computes σ(n) − n (i.e., the aliquot sum s(n)).
- **`HasNaturalDensityZero`**: Standard ε-δ definition of natural density zero: for all ε > 0, eventually |A ∩ [0, x)| / x < ε. Correct.
- **`open scoped Classical`**: Needed for decidability of `(· ∈ A)` in `Finset.filter`. Appropriate.
- **Theorem statement**: Correctly formalizes "density-zero sets are closed under s⁻¹" as a direct assertion.

The formalization faithfully captures the original problem.
