Since $g(n)$ is defined as the *maximum* number of distinct $R$-values over all $n$-point configurations, $g(n) \geq (1-\varepsilon)n$ is equivalent to the *existence* of a configuration achieving $\geq (1-\varepsilon)n$ distinct values, which is exactly what the `∃ x` quantifier captures. The cast from `ℕ` to `ℝ` for `numDistinctR n x` is necessary since `(1 - ε) * n` can be non-integral.
