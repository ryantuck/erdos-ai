### `answer()` usage

The original problem asks two questions:
1. "What is the size of the largest A ⊆ {1,…,N} such that lcm(a,b) ≤ N?" — a **"What is..."** question (Case 2).
2. "Is it attained by choosing all integers in [1, (N/2)^{1/2}] together with all even integers in [(N/2)^{1/2}, (2N)^{1/2}]?" — a **yes/no** question (Case 1).

The formalization uses **direct assertions** (Case 3) for all three theorems:

- [x] `erdos_441`: states the asymptotic upper bound `|A| ≤ (1+ε)√(9N/8)` directly — no `answer()`.
- [x] `erdos_441.variants.lower`: states the asymptotic lower bound directly.
- [x] `erdos_441.variants.disproved`: states directly that Erdős' construction is not always optimal.

> **Discussion:** Strictly, "What is the size..." is Case 2 and "Is it attained..." is Case 1, which would call for `answer()` wrappers. However, the answer to question 1 is an asymptotic equivalence `g(N) ~ (9N/8)^{1/2}` rather than a single value, making a two-sided bound formulation arguably more natural and mathematically precise. For question 2, the answer is "No" (disproved), and the `variants.disproved` theorem captures this as a direct existential statement. The current Case 3 formulation is defensible, but a reviewer may wish to discuss whether an `answer()` wrapper would better match the problem's phrasing.
