### Formalization notes

The formalization is mathematically sound:
- "Almost all" is correctly formalized as natural density zero via the ε-N definition: for every ε > 0, eventually the count of exceptional n in [0, N) is at most ε·N.
- Consecutive primes are represented using `nth Nat.Prime n` and `nth Nat.Prime (n + 1)`.
- The prime gap is `nth Nat.Prime (n + 1) - nth Nat.Prime n` (natural subtraction is safe since primes are increasing).
- The least prime factor condition uses `minFac m`, which is well-defined for the relevant values of m (m > 2 since it must exceed the nth prime).
