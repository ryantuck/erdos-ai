However, the formalization splits the problem into two **direct assertions** (Case 3):
- An upper bound: `τ(G) ≤ r/5` (main theorem).
- A lower bound: existence of `G` with `τ(G) ≥ 3r/16 + 7/8` (variant).

- [x] Both theorems use Case 3 style (direct assertions, no `answer()` wrapper) — consistent with stating proved bounds rather than a single "determine" statement.

**Assessment:** The gap between `3r/16` and `r/5` suggests the exact optimal `t` may still be open. Splitting into two proved bounds and stating them as Case 3 direct assertions is a reasonable and defensible design choice. A single `answer(sorry)` theorem would be awkward here since the precise answer isn't known. **Acceptable.**
