The original problem asks to "characterise those A, B ⊆ ℕ with positive density such that d(A+B) = d(A) + d(B)." This is a "determine/find" type question (Case 2). However, the docstring explicitly explains that the formalization captures one specific direction of the conjectured characterization as a direct assertion — "if d(A+B) = d(A)+d(B) with positive densities, then A and B arise from measurable subsets of [0,1) via an irrational rotation." This makes Case 3 appropriate for the formalized statement.

- [x] The theorem type is `P` directly (no `answer()` wrapper), consistent with Case 3 (direct assertion).
- [ ] **Note:** The original problem asks for a full characterization (both directions), but the formalization only captures one direction (necessary condition). The reverse direction — that sets arising from irrational rotations with the measure additivity property do have additive density — is not stated. This is acknowledged in the docstring but means the formalization is incomplete relative to the original problem.
- [ ] **Note:** The docstring says "irrational rotation" but the formalization only requires `0 < θ` (line 76) without requiring `Irrational θ`. If θ is rational, the construction may degenerate. Consider adding `Irrational θ` as a hypothesis.
