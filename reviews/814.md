**Mathematical notes:**

- The formalization correctly captures the combinatorial content: for every k ≥ 2, there is a constant c > 0 such that for sufficiently large n, any graph on n vertices with at least `(k-1)(n-k+2) + C(k-2,2) + 1` edges contains a nonempty vertex subset S of size at most `(1-c)·n` where every vertex in S has at least k neighbors within S.

- Natural number subtraction in `n - k + 2` and `k - 2` is safe under the given constraints (`k ≥ 2`, `n ≥ N₀` with N₀ chosen suitably).
