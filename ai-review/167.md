## 6. Correctness

The formalization is **mathematically correct and complete**. Detailed analysis:

### Edge-disjointness encoding
Two triangles (3-cliques) in a simple graph share an edge if and only if they share exactly 2 vertices (since every pair of vertices in a triangle forms an edge). Therefore, `(t‚āĀ ‚ą© t‚āā).card ‚Č§ 1` correctly encodes edge-disjointness for triangles. The docstring explicitly notes this reasoning. ‚úď

```lean
  T.card ‚Č§ k
```
This says "every family of pairwise edge-disjoint triangles has size ‚Č§ k," i.e., k ‚Č• őĹ(G). Since k is universally quantified over ‚Ąē, the conjecture is strongest when k = őĹ(G), giving ŌĄ(G) ‚Č§ 2¬∑őĹ(G). For k > őĹ(G), the statement follows from the k = őĹ(G) case. Thus the universal quantification over k is equivalent to Tuza's conjecture. ‚úď

### Triangle covering number (conclusion)

```lean
  H ‚Č§ G ‚ąß
```

The existential `‚ąÉ (H : SimpleGraph V)` does not require `[DecidableRel H.Adj]`. This is fine ‚ÄĒ the statement is purely propositional and decidability of H's adjacency is not needed. ‚úď
