## 6. Correctness

### Main theorem (`erdos_751`) ŌĆö Correct

The formalization states that `answer(False)` holds iff for all $C$, there exists a finite graph $G$ with $\chi(G)=4$ such that all pairs of cycle lengths $m_1 < m_2$ satisfy $C \leq m_2 - m_1$. This quantifies over *all* pairs rather than just *consecutive* pairs (as the original problem states). However, the two formulations are equivalent: if all consecutive gaps are $\geq C$, then any non-consecutive gap $m_j - m_i$ (where $j > i$) is $\geq (j-i) \cdot C \geq C$. So requiring all pairwise gaps $\geq C$ is equivalent to requiring all consecutive gaps $\geq C$. The `answer(False)` direction is correct.
