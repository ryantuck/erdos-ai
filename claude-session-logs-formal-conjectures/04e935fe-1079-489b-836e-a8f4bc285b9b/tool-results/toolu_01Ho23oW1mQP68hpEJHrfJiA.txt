    13‚Üí    13‚Üí# Hausdorff distance
    14‚Üí    14‚Üí
    15‚Üí    15‚ÜíThe Hausdorff distance on subsets of a metric (or emetric) space.
    16‚Üí    16‚Üí
    17‚Üí    17‚ÜíGiven two subsets `s` and `t` of a metric space, their Hausdorff distance is the smallest `d`
    18‚Üí    18‚Üísuch that any point of `s` is within `d` of a point in `t`, and conversely. This quantity
    19‚Üí    19‚Üíis often infinite (think of `s` bounded and `t` unbounded), and therefore better
    20‚Üí    20‚Üíexpressed in the setting of emetric spaces.
    21‚Üí    21‚Üí
    22‚Üí    22‚Üí## Main definitions
    23‚Üí    23‚Üí
    24‚Üí    24‚ÜíThis file introduces:
    25‚Üí    25‚Üí* `Metric.infEDist x s`, the infimum edistance of a point `x` to a set `s` in an emetric space
    26‚Üí    26‚Üí* `Metric.hausdorffEDist s t`, the Hausdorff edistance of two sets in an emetric space
    27‚Üí    27‚Üí* Versions of these notions on metric spaces, called respectively `Metric.infDist`
    28‚Üí    28‚Üí  and `Metric.hausdorffDist`
    29‚Üí    29‚Üí
    30‚Üí    30‚Üí## Main results
    31‚Üí    31‚Üí* `infEDist_closure`: the edistance to a set and its closure coincide
    32‚Üí    32‚Üí* `Metric.mem_closure_iff_infEDist_zero`: a point `x` belongs to the closure of `s` iff
    33‚Üí    33‚Üí  `infEDist x s = 0`
    34‚Üí    34‚Üí* `IsCompact.exists_infEDist_eq_edist`: if `s` is compact and non-empty, there exists a point `y`
    35‚Üí    35‚Üí  which attains this edistance
    36‚Üí    36‚Üí* `IsOpen.exists_iUnion_isClosed`: every open set `U` can be written as the increasing union
    37‚Üí    37‚Üí  of countably many closed subsets of `U`
    38‚Üí    38‚Üí
    39‚Üí    39‚Üí* `hausdorffEDist_closure`: replacing a set by its closure does not change the Hausdorff edistance
    40‚Üí    40‚Üí* `hausdorffEDist_zero_iff_closure_eq_closure`: two sets have Hausdorff edistance zero
    41‚Üí    41‚Üí  iff their closures coincide
    42‚Üí    42‚Üí* the Hausdorff edistance is symmetric and satisfies the triangle inequality
    43‚Üí    43‚Üí* in particular, closed sets in an emetric space are an emetric space
    44‚Üí    44‚Üí  (this is shown in `EMetricSpace.Closeds.emetricSpace`)
    45‚Üí    45‚Üí
    46‚Üí    46‚Üí* versions of these notions on metric spaces
    47‚Üí    47‚Üí* `hausdorffEDist_ne_top_of_nonempty_of_bounded`: if two sets in a metric space
    48‚Üí    48‚Üí  are nonempty and bounded in a metric space, they are at finite Hausdorff edistance.
    49‚Üí    49‚Üí
